⚓ Maritime English Proficiency Test (MEPT)

Grammar | Reading | Writing | Listening | Speaking

✍️ Writing Tasks (30 marks)

Write your answers in the boxes below. Check grammar and maritime terms.

📝 Part 1 (about 25 words) – Choose ONE option

Option A: Hand Tool Safety

Write to a friend about using tools safely on deck. Include: broken tool seen, reported to, why fixing quickly important, safety rule remembered.

Option B: Cold Weather Challenge

Write about working in cold weather during watch. Include: feeling in freezing wind, heavy clothes/drinks, why cold normal on North routes, gear for next time.

📝 Part 2 (80–100 words) – Choose ONE topic

Option A: Keeping Deck Clean

How you maintain housekeeping, cleaning oily surfaces, focus tasks, avoid slipping, why clean workspace supports safety.

Option B: Learning Ship Routines

Adapting to daily schedule, waking early, mastering routines, showing dedication, following orders, why quick learning helpful.

🎧 Listening Comprehension (25 marks)

Read the transcripts (or listen to audio if provided) and answer True/False/Doesn't Say.

🗣️ Speaking Test (In-person – 15 minutes)

Practice with a partner or record yourself. Topics:

  • Introduce yourself (rank, duties, experience)
  • Describe a fire drill procedure
  • Give navigational commands ("Steer 270 degrees")
  • Report an engine problem using SMCP
  • Explain enclosed space entry safety

Self-assessment: Speak clearly, use correct maritime vocabulary.
